5 Common Mistakes New Traders Make

By John Doe | March 1, 2025 | 5 min read

Trading can be an exciting journey, but beginners often fall into traps that cost them dearly. Here are the five most common mistakes and how to avoid them.

1. No Trading Plan

Entering trades without a clear plan is like sailing without a map. Define your entry, exit, and risk management rules before each trade.

2. Risking Too Much

Risking more than 1-2% of your account per trade can wipe you out quickly. Stick to proper position sizing.

3. Overtrading

More trades don't mean more profit. Quality over quantity is key.

4. Ignoring Risk/Reward

Always aim for a risk/reward ratio of at least 1:2 to stay profitable over time.

5. Letting Emotions Rule

Fear and greed are the biggest enemies. Stick to your plan and use a trading journal to review your performance.
